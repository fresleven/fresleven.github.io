About

I a first year Ph.D. student in Computer Science at University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and a National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellow (NSF GRFP). I develop machine learning methods for high-energy physics and geospatial science. My research addresses challenges in reliable ML: uncertainty quantification, out-of-distribution detection, and causality.

My contributions include:

  • Robust uncertainty quantification techniques [MLST'25, NeurIPS ML4PS Workshop '24]
  • XAI methods [MLST'23]
  • Causal inference [Preprint'25]

My goal is to develop transparent and effective models for real-world deployment, with a focus on high-energy physics and geospatial applications. If you would like to collaborate on any of these topics and beyond, please reach out!

I'm free during summer 2026 and open to exciting opportunities in academia and industry!

Bio

I hold dual bachelor's degrees in Physics and Computer Science from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. During my undergrad, I interned at Argonne and Brookhaven National Laboratory. I am also a contributor to NVIDIA PhysicsNeMo. I like listening to music and playing my violin as well!
